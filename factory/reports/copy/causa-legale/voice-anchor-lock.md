### §2.3 · Why candidate #7 won

**Axis A (em-word semantic family)**: `evidenza` lands on the public-record-evidence sense in IT (`l'evidenza in atti · l'evidenza incardinata in un fascicolo processuale · l'evidenza preliminare`) — the forensic register where the noun reads "the evidence on the record," NOT "the obvious thing" (its everyday Italian sense of `è evidenza che…`). The forensic register is the dominant register in legal-press writing (Foro Italiano · Giurisprudenza Italiana · Rivista di Diritto Civile) — the em on the noun reads as the firm's method-statement, not as a tag-line.

**Axis B (recurrence-tolerance across 2 surfaces)**: the sentence's structure is `X is Y, not Z` — a forensic-press syllogism. The 2nd surface recurrence on cta-closer h2 reads as the **conclusion** of a 600-word narrative argument: the narrative essay between the 2 surfaces deploys 7 different em-words (`giurisdizione · massima · sostenuta · incardina · incardinata · giurisprudenza · principio`) that PROGRESSIVELY pay off the anchor `evidenza` from different angles. The 2nd surface's recurrence reads "the firm has just made the argument; the anchor is the conclusion," not "the firm repeated a slogan."

**Axis C (5-locale translation safety)**: `evidenza` carries verbatim-in-translation cognates that all land on the public-record-evidence sense:
- IT `evidenza` (forensic register)
- EN `evidence` (UK/US legal register: "evidence on the record")
- FR `preuve` (French Cassation register: "preuve versée au dossier")
- ES `evidencia` (Spanish forensic register: "evidencia en el expediente")
- AR `دليل` (MENA legal-press register: "دليل مُثبَت في السجل")

The semantic drift risk is lowest for `evidenza` among all candidates because the public-record-evidence sense is a shared category in continental civil-law systems (IT/FR/ES) and in the AR forensic-press tradition. The EN cognate `evidence` is the SAME noun across UK common-law and US common-law forensic register.

**Axis D (non-collapse vs Cornice)**: `evidenza` is in a categorically different semantic family from `argomento`:
- `argomento` = curatorial-thesis · "the argument the work proposes" · architectural-press register · "the work IS the argument"
- `evidenza` = public-record-evidence · "the evidence the record incardinates" · forensic-press register · "we plead what is incardinated"

Per AC-3 explicit non-cognate test: `argomento / argument / argumento / حُجَّة` is the curatorial-thesis cognate set; `evidenza / evidence / preuve / evidencia / دليل` is the public-record-evidence cognate set. The two sets DO NOT overlap on any of the 5 locales (see §3 below).

## §3 · Why `evidenza` does NOT collapse into Cornice's `argomento`

This is the load-bearing argument of the lock. The two anchor nouns must read as **categorically different** at 1-second eye-track in all 5 locales. The argument has four legs.

### §3.1 · Semantic-family difference

| Axis | Cornice (`argomento`) | Causa (`evidenza`) |
|---|---|---|
| **Register** | architectural-press (Casabella · Domus · Architectural Review · L'Architecture d'Aujourd'hui · Arquitectura Viva) | forensic-press (Foro Italiano · Giurisprudenza Italiana · Rivista di Diritto Civile · Cass. civ. III decisions verbatim) |
| **Disciplinary tradition** | curatorial-architectural · "the argument a built work proposes" | forensic-litigation · "the evidence a sentence incardinates" |
| **What is being claimed** | the studio designs ARGUMENTS (theses constructed on a site, a commission, a constraint) — proof = the published built work | the studio pleads EVIDENCE (proof incardinated in a fascicolo, a sentenza, a massima) — proof = the cited public record |
| **Audience verb** | `read` (the visitor evaluates a curatorial argument) | `plead` (the visitor evaluates a litigation pleading) |
| **Em-word structure** | `argomento costruito` (built argument · architectural metaphor) | `evidenza incardinata` (incardinated evidence · forensic metaphor) |

The two nouns are NOT synonyms in any locale. A reader who lands on Cornice expects "this studio writes architectural arguments." A reader who lands on Causa expects "this firm pleads sentences in court." The two expectations are incompatible — neither studio could swap its anchor without breaking its identity.

### §3.2 · The verb-family difference

Cornice's verb-family (per AC-3 + planner-brief §3.2 + Cornice-pack §D1):
`argomentare · costruire l'argomento · firmare l'argomento · pubblicare l'argomento in collana · scrivere l'argomento del fascicolo`

Causa's verb-family (per planner-brief §1 + this lock §1):
`incardinare · sostenere · patrocinare · depositare · invocare · proporre · impugnare · ricorrere · eccepire · controdedurre`

The two verb-families are DISJOINT. Cornice's verbs ALL belong to architectural-press authoring; Causa's verbs ALL belong to forensic-litigation procedure. A reader who hears "lo studio sostiene ciò che incardina nel fascicolo processuale" cannot mistake the firm for an architecture studio; a reader who hears "lo studio scrive l'argomento in fascicolo" cannot mistake the firm for a Cassazionista.

### §3.3 · The structural sentence-shape difference (rhetorical contrast pair)

Cornice's anchor: `Ogni progetto è un argomento costruito, non un servizio reso.`

Causa's anchor: `Ogni sentenza è un'evidenza incardinata, non un'opinione difesa.`

Both sentences use the rhetorical structure `X is Y, not Z` (the forensic syllogism · the architectural manifesto). The structure is family-shared at the LF-2 family level (per AC-15 voice-anchor-recurrence rule). But the **values of X · Y · Z** are categorically different:

| Slot | Cornice | Causa | Disjoint? |
|---|---|---|---|
| X (subject) | `progetto` (architectural project) | `sentenza` (court ruling) | YES |
| Y (positive predicate) | `argomento costruito` (built argument) | `evidenza incardinata` (incardinated evidence) | YES |
| Z (negative predicate) | `servizio reso` (service rendered) | `opinione difesa` (defended opinion) | YES |

All three slots are disjoint. The structural shape-share is intentional family register (per AC-15); the content-share is zero. A reader cannot conflate the two anchors any more than they can conflate "every ruling is incardinated evidence" with "every project is a built argument."

### §3.4 · The 5-locale verbatim-in-translation lock makes the difference permanent

The verbatim-in-translation contract (per planner-brief §11) means: the em-word moves with the public-record-evidence sense in every locale, NEVER the curatorial-thesis sense. Even if a translator mishears the IT source, the binding contract forces the EN/FR/ES/AR translation to land on `evidence/preuve/evidencia/دليل` and never on `argument/topic/thesis/claim/case-as-argument`.

| Locale | Cornice (binding) | Causa (binding) | Cognate-overlap risk |
|---|---|---|---|
| IT | `argomento` · curatorial-thesis | `evidenza` · public-record-evidence | ZERO · different morphological roots |
| EN | `argument` (architecture-press cognate) | `evidence` (forensic legal cognate) | ZERO · UK/US legal-press register treats `evidence` and `argument` as DIFFERENT categories |
| FR | `argument` (Architecture d'Aujourd'hui register) | `preuve` (Cassation register · "preuve versée au dossier") | ZERO · French translates Cornice's `argomento` as `argument` and Causa's `evidenza` as `preuve` — two different lexemes |
| ES | `argumento` (Arquitectura Viva register) | `evidencia` (forensic register) | LOW · Spanish has both lexemes available; the brief at workflow C locks the translator-binding to `evidencia` (NOT `argumento`) and the back-translation test at workflow D verifies |
| AR | `حُجَّة` (architectural-press register · curatorial-thesis) | `دليل` (forensic-press register · evidence-on-the-record) | ZERO · MENA legal-press treats `حُجَّة` (argument · thesis) and `دليل` (evidence · proof) as categorically different forensic categories |

The 5-locale lock is the strongest non-collapse argument: across 5 languages and 4 different writing systems, `evidenza`'s cognate set NEVER overlaps with `argomento`'s cognate set. A reader landing on either firm's home in any of 5 locales cannot conflate them at the anchor level.

## §4 · Why `evidenza` is strong enough for EN/FR/ES/AR rollout

Workflow C will translate the locked IT anchor into 4 additional locales while preserving the verbatim-in-translation contract. The strength argument has three legs.

### §4.1 · The cognate set is dictionary-stable across continental civil-law systems

`evidenza · evidence · preuve · evidencia · دليل` is one of the few legal-press cognate sets where:
- The IT lexeme is dictionary-equivalent to a single lexeme in each of the other 4 locales (no synonym-cluster ambiguity).
- All 5 lexemes carry the **public-record-evidence** sense as their PRIMARY forensic register (not as a secondary metaphorical use).
- All 5 lexemes are **morphologically stable** (the lexeme stays the same across morphological inflections — `evidenza/evidenze/evidenziale` · `evidence/evidential` · `preuve/preuves/probant` · `evidencia/evidencias/evidencial` · `دليل/أدلة/إثباتي` — the em-word always lands on the noun root).

By contrast, alternative anchor candidates carry translation-fragility:
- `prova` / `proof` / `preuve` / `prueba` / `إثبات` — `إثبات` (proof) and `دليل` (evidence) drift apart in MENA legal register; the EN `proof` and `evidence` carry different shades; the IT `prova` is closer to "proof of fact" than to "evidence on the record."
- `fascicolo` / `dossier` / `dossier` / `expediente` / `ملف` — `dossier` collides with Cornice's CTA mental model in EN/FR; `expediente` works but the recurrence reads thinner.
- `massima` / `maxim` / `maxime` / `máxima` / `قاعدة` — `قاعدة` carries Quranic-law connotation in AR; `maxim` reads archaic in EN; the EN/FR cognates are typographically thinner.

`evidenza` is the only candidate that clears all 4 axes (semantic stability + morphological stability + register-primary + non-cognate vs Cornice) across all 5 locales.

### §4.2 · The verbatim sentence translates cleanly with em-word preserved

The 5-locale verbatim translations of the anchor sentence (workflow C source · planner-brief §11):

| Locale | Anchor sentence (verbatim · em on the public-record-evidence cognate) |
|---|---|
| **IT** | `Ogni sentenza è un'<em>evidenza</em> incardinata, non un'opinione difesa.` |
| **EN** | `Every ruling is <em>evidence</em> on the record — not an opinion defended.` |
| **FR** | `Chaque décision est une <em>preuve</em> versée au dossier — non une opinion défendue.` |
| **ES** | `Cada sentencia es una <em>evidencia</em> incardinada — no una opinión defendida.` |
| **AR** | `كلُّ حكمٍ <em>دليلٌ</em> مُثبَتٌ في السجل، لا رأيٌ يُدافَع عنه.` |

Each translation:
- Preserves the em-word on the noun root in the public-record-evidence sense.
- Preserves the rhetorical contrast structure (`X is Y, not Z`).
- Preserves the verbatim-on-2-surfaces recurrence (the same translated sentence lands on hero h1 + cta-closer h2).
- Reads as a method-statement in the locale's forensic register, not as a marketing-slogan.

The AR translation specifically:
- Uses `حكم` (ruling · decision · verdict) for "sentenza" — the standard MENA legal-press lexeme.
- Uses `دليل` (evidence on the record) — explicitly NOT `شهادة` (testimony), NOT `قرينة` (circumstantial proof), NOT `إثبات` (proof). The forensic register's primary lexeme.
- Uses `مُثبَتٌ في السجل` (incardinated in the record) — explicitly NOT `محفوظ` (preserved · archived) which is closer to Continua's stewardship register.
- Uses `لا رأيٌ يُدافَع عنه` (not an opinion defended) — the negation structure mirrors the IT.

The Naskh AR h1 swap (per LF-2 AC-13 · `body.cs-lf-lf-2`-scoped) lands the AR anchor on a forensic-publication serif (Naskh) instead of the cluster-default monumental Kufi — matching the editorial-publication register Cornice established and Causa inherits.

### §4.3 · The translator binding contract (workflow C)

The translator brief at workflow C carries the verbatim-in-translation contract as a binding rule:

1. **The em-word must land on the public-record-evidence sense** in every locale (NOT proof-as-statistics · NOT testimony · NOT clue · NOT circumstantial-proof). Translator's choice of cognate is bound to the public-record-evidence sense; alternative renderings are REJECTED.

2. **The em-word must be the same lexeme on the hero h1 and the cta-closer h2** in every locale. The 2-surface verbatim recurrence is non-negotiable (per AC-15).

3. **The em-word must NOT collide with Cornice's `argomento` cognate set** in any locale (per AC-3 explicit non-cognate test). The translator rejects any rendering that brings the em-word into the curatorial-thesis cognate semantic family.

4. **The em-word must NOT collide with Continua's `generazioni` cognate set** in any locale (per planner-brief §3.5 hard-ban). The translator rejects any rendering that brings the em-word into the stewardship-temporal cognate semantic family.

5. **The em-word must read as forensic-press register** (NOT general-purpose legal · NOT consumer-facing legal · NOT decorative-Latin) in every locale. The Foro Italiano / EN UK&US legal-press / Cass. de France / Tribunal Supremo / MENA legal-press register is the binding reference.

These 5 binding rules are written into the workflow C translator brief verbatim. The back-translation test at workflow D verifies each translation independently before the public flip.
