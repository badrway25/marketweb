## 5 · Top 5 differentiation moves (the moves that make Cornice read as the 5th identity)

In descending order of how much each move buys at the first 30 seconds of read.

1. **Stacked-editorial hero with a project-credit overlay carrying 3 inline KPI stats** — the hero is a full-bleed photograph of a completed building (no human, no desk, no library, no boardroom — a building) with `(Project name · Year · Committenza tipo)` overlaid as a publication-credit, plus three inline stats (`12 opere realizzate · 3 città · 28 anni di pratica`). Every existing sibling renders 55/45 split or object-overlay; Cornice's hero is stacked-vertical with photo TOP and h1 + side-quoted intro BELOW in 8/4 col. First-30s read flips from "advisory firm intro" to "monograph spread" the moment the page loads. This is the single most decisive move.
2. **Magazine-grid cases (LF-2 L7) with each case rendered as a photographed project, not a typographic row** — Pragma/Fiscus/Solaria/Continua all render cases as list-row entries (numbered rows · text-only at home) or timeline (Continua). Cornice ships a 1+3 magazine grid: one large card with the studio's signature project + three smaller cards. Every card carries a project image. Cases are visible work, not anonymized stories. This is the second most decisive move and the one that makes Cornice's archive feel like a public-record monograph rather than a confidential portfolio.
3. **Single-portrait-feature leadership (LF-2 L6) — one masthead portrait of the founding architect** — Pragma+Fiscus are typographic-only, Solaria has a 1+1 photo-pair, Continua has a 3-card pillar-photo. Cornice ships ONE portrait, masthead-sized, with a 2-paragraph bio and credentials. The architect's career *is* the firm's portfolio; flattening into a 3-card grid would erase the principal–associate hierarchy. This move makes the firm read as "led by someone" rather than "staffed by partners."
4. **Editorial-curatorial voice anchor on a spatial noun** — `"Ogni opera istituzionale risponde al suo <em>luogo</em>."` ("Every institutional work answers to its place.") Italic falls on `luogo` — a **spatial** noun, the first in the cluster. Pragma's italic = decisional/agency word; Fiscus's = imperative; Solaria's = contrast-pair nouns; Continua's = temporal noun (`generazioni`). Cornice's italic on a SPATIAL noun says: this is about cities and sites, not advice or custody. The voice positioning that follows is editorial-curatorial — the firm reads as a publication that builds, not as an advisor that consults.
5. **Graphite + travertine + archive-burgundy macro palette · cool/warm/warm polarity** — orthogonal to Pragma (cool/cool/cool · navy+blue+emerald), Fiscus (cool/warm/cool · gray-blue+gold+blunotte), Solaria (warm/warm/warm · carbon+ocra+caramel), Continua (cool/cool/warm · pine+pewter+brass). Cornice claims **cool/warm/warm** (graphite primary `#23262A` · travertine secondary `#D9CFB8` · archive-burgundy accent `#6E2A2D`), the only OPEN polarity strategy after Continua's landing. Travertine reads "limestone façade / Roman civic" — a non-blue, non-gold, non-pewter neutral that has not appeared in the cluster. Archive-burgundy reads "Veneto leather binding / monograph cover," a saturated warm distinct from Solaria's caramel (more orange) and Fiscus's gold (more yellow).

A 6th visible diff worth noting: **Cormorant Garamond + Work Sans** as typography pair. Cormorant Garamond is the open editorial-monograph serif (matrix §1.4 OPEN list); Work Sans is a non-Inter humanist body sans. Together they read "publication / monograph / Wallpaper magazine," carrying the LF-2 voice gravity at the type level. This is below the top 5 because it lands on second-scroll rather than first-30s, but is binding nonetheless (Inter as body would collapse the cluster — three uses across Pragma+Solaria+a 5th Inter sibling would erase typography differentiation).

## 6 · Top 5 risks (named, mitigated, with explicit fallback moves)

These are the dimensions where Cornice sits closer to a sibling than ideal. Each is mitigated below; the build phase verifies the mitigation lands. **Each carries a fallback move** — the action the orchestrator routes to if the mitigation does not survive the live render.

### Risk 1 · Stacked-editorial hero photo (full-bleed building exterior) reading as Pragma's atrium feature

- **The overlap**: Pragma's about/feature slots include a "corporate atrium" image. Cornice's hero is a building exterior at dusk, but the slot_1 narrative photo (atrium interior · monumental staircase · travertine columns) sits topologically close to Pragma's atrium. Two atriums at home/about pages can read as the same firm at first scroll.
- **Why this is not a collision (paper level)**: Pragma's atrium image appears at slot_4 of its imagery pack as feature/about-band — small surface area, conference-room context, people in frame. Cornice's atrium appears at slot_1 NARRATIVE band on home — larger surface area, monumental civic register, **zero** people in frame, single-architectural-element framing (one staircase ascending, columns flanking). The two reads as "boardroom backdrop" vs "civic monument."
- **Mitigation binding**: A.3 curator hard-rejects any slot_1 candidate showing > 0 people, any conference-room furniture (chairs, tables, screens), any laptop/keyboard. The atrium must read as room-monumental (single ascending staircase + columns + skylight) NOT room-occupied (people-walking-through framing). Cross-cluster grep against `business-corporate` is mandatory before URL commit.
- **Walk gate to verify**: B-IMG-COH-1 (imagery coherence) at 1280 + 1024 (where atrium photos most often crop into "ordinary office lobby"). Style-critic at A.6 confirms slot_1 reads "civic monument" not "office lobby."
- **Fallback if overlap appears at A.7**: re-curate slot_1 only, narrower keywords ("travertine atrium staircase" without "office" or "lobby"), pick the candidate with the highest column-count and lowest furniture-density. Single A.3 curator pass · 30 minutes. Hard stop: do NOT replace atrium with another building exterior — the narrative band needs one interior shot to vary the macro mood from the hero exterior.

### Risk 2 · Architecture-firm "render-as-photo" Pexels miss

- **The overlap**: Pexels has a non-trivial volume of architecture-render images (not photographs) that pass casual visual inspection but read as "AI-generated SaaS render" on close look. The cluster's anti-AI-slop rule (`§4.6` + CS reference-pack §9) bans render imagery. The risk is silent — the curator picks a render thinking it's a photo, the photo reads as ordinary at first, but a discerning viewer (or a discerning style-critic at A.6) flags it.
- **Why this is not a collision (paper level)**: Cornice's brief states explicitly "real completed building" not "rendered concept." The brand contract says photographs only.
- **Mitigation binding**: A.3 curator runs a **render-vs-photo gate** on every architecture image: (i) does the image contain at least one human-scale element shot in real light (a person at distance, a real shadow at golden-hour angle, real weather, real worn material)? (ii) are reflections in glass plausible (real sky / real cloud movement / real adjacent buildings) rather than ray-traced-clean? (iii) is the architectural detail age-consistent (signs of real weathering, not pristine post-render finish)? If any of the three gates fails, the candidate is rejected. The gate is documented in the slot's `rejection_rules_for_curator` block in `cornice-build-brief.md §4`.
- **Walk gate to verify**: A.6 style-critic runs the same 3-gate check on the rendered home page. A.7 walker re-runs at 1920 + 1280 + 720; render-imagery most often gives itself away at large viewport where details show.
- **Fallback if a render passes the gate and gets surfaced at A.7**: pull the image, narrow the search lane ("photograph" prefix added to query), prefer images with visible patina / weathering / human-scale signal. Re-walk on the affected page only.
