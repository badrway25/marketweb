## §4 · Should this live inside an existing LF family or require LF-NEW

**LF-2 second occupant.** Not LF-6, not LF-{NEW}.

The argument:

1. **The fit matches LF-2's reference profile to the line.** `corporate-suite-live-family-map.md §4` specifies LF-2 second-occupant is allowed for "portfolio-of-work-led firm whose proof IS its case studies (evidence-led litigation · independent directorship case-bundle · audit-led methodology with published methodology pieces · longitudinal research practice with publications)." Evidence-led litigation is the first item on that list. The fit is not a stretch — it is the canonical second-occupant case.

2. **The family is documented end-to-end.** Cornice's `cornice-lf2-reference-pack.md` enumerates AC-1..AC-16 (anti-collapse rules), §5 (don't-copy table), §6 (inherit-verbatim table), §7 (LF-2 walk gates), §8 (R-LF2-1..8 risk register), §9 (intake questions). The 6th sibling has a recipe; the planner's first read is a complete document.

3. **LF-6 first-occupant would be the wrong order of operations.** Activating LF-6 with the 6th sibling means binding the provisional L1–L9 tuple at the same intake — adding a hardening dependency to what should be a pure recipe execution. LF-2 second-occupant validates that the **family system is repeatable** before testing whether it is **extensible**. Validate repeatability first; extend later (sibling 7 or 8, not 6).

4. **LF-{NEW} is not warranted.** A litigation boutique does not require a new layout grammar. Its proof is case-bundle; LF-2 was built for exactly that proof shape. Choosing LF-{NEW} here would be over-claiming a difference that is in fact a sub-cluster variation inside an existing family.

The 6th sibling is the LF-2 family's repeatability test. That test is overdue and well-prepared.
