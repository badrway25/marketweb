## Cross-pattern observation · the patterns interact, not compete

Pattern families 1-8 are not orthogonal. A template under the corporate-suite
cluster contract is bound to ALL of them simultaneously:

- Pattern 1 (hero) constrains Pattern 4 (CTA) — a serif italic-em hero CTA
  is naturally a polite-imperative + abstract-noun.
- Pattern 5 (motion) constrains Pattern 7 (premium trope) — restraint is
  the trope, restraint is the motion.
- Pattern 6 (imagery) constrains Pattern 8 (audience verb) — composed-
  restraint photography is naturally read by a deliberative audience.
- Pattern 2 (section rhythm) constrains Pattern 3 (proof) — the atom set
  determines what proof tactics are expressible.

Trying to break ONE pattern in isolation under the cluster contract is
load-bearing-painful: the next sibling's planner-brief would have to
enumerate the deviation, justify it at A.6, and re-walk distinctness vs all
existing siblings on the same axis. **Trying to break MULTIPLE patterns at
once means leaving the cluster.**

This is why the audit's recommendation is **not** "break the CTA pattern
inside corporate-suite" or "add a 7th sibling with a non-deliberative verb"
— those moves would force one or two pattern-breaks inside an envelope that
binds eight. The recommendation is to ship a different cluster, where the
envelope is set differently from the start.
