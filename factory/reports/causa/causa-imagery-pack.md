## §3 · The hardest slot

**Slot 0 (hero · empty courtroom interior · cool light · vertical timber + bone walls · zero people)** is the hardest slot in this pack. The reasons (per `causa-prebuild-quick-checks.md §3` + curator's lived experience at A.3 entry):

1. **Pexels pool is genuinely thin**. The "courtroom" search on Pexels skews heavily toward (a) gavel close-ups, (b) lawyer-and-client conversation poses, (c) Lady Justice statues, (d) library/books-on-desk shots — all of which fail the binding triple (zero-people + interior-architectural + cool-timber-and-bone). Cool-lit empty European/Italian court interiors are a thin sub-pool. The curator's primary search returned **3-4 plausible candidates** at premium quality (≥1600 width · landscape · clearing the binding triple) — barely above the planner's escape-hatch threshold.

2. **The collapse risks are concentrated here**. Slot 0 is where Continua adjacency (interior-warm-mahogany), Cornice adjacency (golden-hour exterior), Pragma adjacency (boardroom long-table), and the cluster's general "books-on-desk" stewardship-cliché all meet. The planner-brief §13.6 hard-ban list applies URL-by-URL — most candidate URLs fail at least one hard ban.

3. **Hero geometry constraint** (LF-2 L1=stacked-editorial · 16:9 landscape · KPI tuple in bottom-left credit-overlay): the hero photo must (a) work full-bleed at 1920/1440/1280/1100, (b) carry the hero-overlay KPI tuple in its bottom-left luminance region with AA contrast, (c) survive the 4:3 ↔ 16:9 crop swap, (d) read at 1100+ as the 8/4 below-fold split picks up the typographic side. The empty-courtroom subject must hold all four constraints simultaneously.

**Mitigation in this pack**:
- **4 hero backups in entries 11-14** (entry 14 is the planner-pre-cleared codex-spread fallback authority — not invoked at this curator pass but live for A.5/A.6 re-curate).
- **Slot 0 weakness disclosure** in `business-litigation.md` slot 0 caption block (per the brief's "no fake certainty" instruction).
- **A.5 live-verification gate**: build commits to fetch slot 0 primary, re-test the 3-second subject read against the binding rules, and substitute from backups 11-13 (or fallback to entry 14 codex-spread) if subject-fit fails. This is a procedural escalation, NOT a re-spec.
- **A.6 style-critic gate**: re-runs the binding-triple test on the rendered home page at 1920/1280/1100. If the rendered slot 0 collapses into Continua reading-room or Cornice portico at 1-second read, re-curate from backups 11-13.

**The second-hardest slot** is slot 2 (portrait · founder · single-portrait masthead). Per the Cornice precedent (`pool-selection.md §7`: "for future LF-2 occupants treat the single-portrait slot as the load-bearing curation surface, not the detail"), the single-portrait surface concentrates the cluster's leadership presence into ONE image — there is no second portrait to balance a marginal first portrait. The R-LF2-1 LinkedIn-headshot collapse risk is real on Pexels' "senior lawyer" search return. Mitigation: 4 portrait-2 backups (entries 17-20) covering gender axis (entry 17 feminine path-b), composition axis (entry 18 standing-at-shelf), age axis (entry 19 60s+), and ethnicity axis (entry 20).

The pack therefore documents both slots as load-bearing and provides depth at both — but slot 0 is the empirically hardest at A.3 entry due to the thin Pexels sub-pool.
