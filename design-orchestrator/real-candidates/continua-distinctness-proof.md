## 4 · Visible differentiators (the moves that make Continua read as the 4th identity)

If a stakeholder spent 30 seconds on each of the four pages (Pragma · Fiscus · Solaria · Continua), these are the moves that make Continua read as a 4th distinct identity rather than a recoloured 3rd.

1. **Object-led hero with zero people** — the moment a viewer sees Pragma/Fiscus/Solaria all having visible humans (boardroom · implied solo · 1:1) and Continua showing a reading room alone, the cluster's silhouette breaks open. This is the single most decisive move.

2. **Pine + brass instead of any blue or any earth tone** — the cluster has been cool-blue (Pragma), warm-gold (Fiscus), warm-earth (Solaria). Pine green is the first cool-non-blue primary. Brass is warm but reads "weighed metal" not "tech green" or "ocra." Stakeholders cycling through the four read palette as the second-strongest distinguisher.

3. **The mid-strip names a CADENCE, not a number or a calendar event** — Continua's mid-strip says "Cadenza CdF · 4 riunioni / anno + Audit di continuità · annuale + Patto familiare · revisione triennale." Fiscus's says "scadenza fiscale 31 marzo." Solaria's says "inizio dichiarato → cadenza → fine dichiarata." Pragma has none. Continua introduces a NEW kind of mid-strip beat: the firm's RHYTHM rather than a deadline or a process arc.

4. **The CTA names a DIALOGUE, not a call** — "Avvia un dialogo di mandato" reads as the start of a multi-year conversation, not a meeting on Tuesday. Pragma/Fiscus/Solaria all sell a call/appointment; Continua sells the opening of a relationship.

5. **Public Sans in the body** — the moment a body-text comparison happens, Continua's Public Sans reads visibly different from Pragma/Solaria's Inter and Fiscus's Plex Sans. The cluster's "Inter on whatever serif" risk is closed.

6. **Crimson Pro h1 italic on a single temporal noun** — Pragma's italic falls on agency words ("decisioni"), Fiscus's on imperatives ("adempimento"), Solaria's on contrast nouns ("terapia"/"consulenza"). Continua's italic falls on a TIME word ("generazioni"). The italic emphasis carries the firm's defining axis (time horizon), not its method or its product.

7. **Leadership block with 60s + 40s + visible diversity, photo-present** — the only photo-present leadership precedent is Solaria's (30sCx2 demographic gap). Continua takes the precedent forward AND fixes the gap — visible age + gender + ethnicity diversity becomes the differentiator within the same precedent.

8. **Cases labelled "Mandati in continuità" with multi-year duration markers** — Pragma's NDA-anonymized + Fiscus's "Casi seguiti" + Solaria's "Casi anonimizzati" all read as past-tense closure. Continua's "Mandati in continuità" reads as ongoing, multi-year, with explicit duration markers ("4ª generazione · da 12 anni"). The label itself signals time-axis instead of outcome-list.

9. **Whistleblowing link prominent in the legal row** — required for advisory clusters by D.lgs. 24/2023 (CS-FOOT-02). Continua's footer treats this as a first-class link, not a footnote — signals the public-trust posture that family-office stewardship requires.

10. **The "remove the studio name" test passes for a stewardship office specifically** — delete "Continua" from every surface and the page still reads as a real stewardship family office, not as a generic "premium firm." The vocabulary (CdF, custodi, patto familiare, generazioni, mandato in continuità, Albo Trustees, STEP) is structurally distinct from the boardroom · commercialista · coaching vocabularies of the other three.

## 5 · Risky overlaps (named, mitigated, with explicit fallback moves)

These are dimensions where Continua sits closer to a sibling than ideal. Each is mitigated below; the build phase must verify the mitigation lands. **Each carries an explicit FALLBACK MOVE** — the action the orchestrator routes to if the mitigation does not survive the live render.

### Risk 1 · Cool-on-cool palette echo of Pragma (palette adjacency)

- **The overlap**: Pragma is full-cool (navy + cool-blue + emerald). Continua is cool/cool/warm (pine + pewter + brass). Two cool-primary palettes side by side at 1920 first scroll can read as the same family if the warm accent does not land prominently.
- **Why this is not a collision (paper level)**: Pragma's is FULL cool · Continua's introduces a warm accent (brass) which Pragma does not have. The brass is the chrome's only colored interactive element (CTA · focus ring) and is highly visible. A first-30s read of Pragma vs Continua is "navy/emerald institutional advisory" vs "pine/brass stewardship office" — the warm accent lands as the differentiator.
- **Mitigation binding**: brass MUST appear at ≥ 5 viewport touchpoints (build-brief §7 brass touchpoint inventory): trailing nav CTA · focus-visible ring · KPI eyebrow tints · pillar card brass underline · cta-closer fill.
- **Walk gate to verify**: contrast report at 1920/1280/720 confirms brass accent visible against pine + cream both. Style-critic at A.6 confirms 5 brass touchpoints render at 1920 first scroll.
- **Fallback move if overlap appears at A.7**: routed by the orchestrator at A.8, narrowest possible diff. Two options ranked:
  1. **Increase brass saturation** by ~6% chroma (within the brass family, not a colour change) — the load-bearing brass touchpoints become more visually distinct from pine. Single-CSS-token edit; re-walk on the affected viewport only.
  2. **Add a 6th brass touchpoint** at the pillars section divider (currently pewter) — increases the brass count to 6 visible per viewport, improves brass:pine ratio at 1280/720 where chrome compresses.
- **Hard stop**: do NOT route to changing pine to a more-saturated green or to changing brass to gold. Both moves break the matrix §1.3 OPEN warmth strategy. If neither fallback above lands the brass, surface as `[BLOCKING]` and route to A.2 re-spec on the palette — not a patch.

### Risk 2 · Object-led hero reading too close to Fiscus's desk-with-documents

- **The overlap**: Fiscus's hero is tidy desk + documents (interior-warm · object-adjacent). Continua's hero is library/partner-study reading room (interior-warm · object-led). Both are interior-warm with desk furniture in frame. The mood overlap is real if the curator picks a hero photo with multiple documents or visible laptop.
- **Why this is not a collision (paper level)**: Fiscus shows MULTIPLE document layers (papers, laptop, eyeglasses) framed at desk-task scale. Continua shows a SINGLE archival object (a book on a desk, with shelves in the background) framed at room-architectural scale. Fiscus reads "tax pro at work today" · Continua reads "stewardship office across years."
- **Mitigation binding**: A.3 curator hard-rejects any hero candidate showing > 1 document, any laptop/keyboard/monitor, any eyeglasses-on-paperwork. The hero must read as room-architectural (single book + lamp · shelves in soft focus) NOT desk-task (multiple papers). The build-brief §4 imagery DNA carries this rejection rule explicitly.
- **Walk gate to verify**: imagery coherence check at 1280 + 1024 (where object-led photos most often crop into incoherence). A.7 walk runs the "would this still be the right photo here if you were building this template from scratch" test on every image at every viewport.
- **Fallback move if overlap appears at A.7**:
  1. **Re-curate hero only** (not the whole pack): pull the lead candidate, re-search with stricter keywords ("library reading room oak shelves" excluding "desk"), pick a frame where shelves dominate and the desk is incidental. Single A.3 curator pass on slot_0 only · 30 minutes.
  2. **Reframe the hero crop**: if the live photo is right but the rendered crop pulls too tight on the desk, adjust `object-position` / responsive `srcset` so the room-architectural frame survives at 1280/1024. CSS-only fix · re-walk on the two viewports.
  3. **Swap to slot 5 ambient as hero candidate** (slate stairwell): if the reading-room subject proves Pexels-thin under the no-document filter, escalate slot 5 to hero, find a new stairwell-adjacent ambient. Last resort because it changes the macro mood.
- **Hard stop**: do NOT route to "make Continua's photo people-led" — that breaks the §4 visible differentiator #1 and collapses the hero distinctness move.
