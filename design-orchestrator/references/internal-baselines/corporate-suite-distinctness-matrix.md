## 0 · How to read this matrix

For each dimension below:
- **Pragma / Cornice / Fiscus / Solaria / Continua columns** = what is locked in today across the 5 live siblings. The next sibling cannot collide on any of these cells.
- **What is already distinct** = where the five siblings successfully differ. Inherit the differentiation strategy.
- **Where overlap is too high** = the load-bearing risk. Where the next sibling is most likely to silently collapse into a sibling rename.
- **What the next sibling MUST NOT repeat** = explicit ban list. Five entries per row (one per existing sibling). The planner brief copies this row verbatim into the new template's content-module docstring as anti-drift binding.

A row that says "MUST NOT repeat: [Pragma's KPI tuple] · [Cornice's argomento anchor] · …" means: the new sibling's planner cannot ship a hero meta-strip composed of years/clients/€/% AND cannot ship a curatorial-thesis voice anchor on `argomento` or its cognates AND etc.

The matrix is checked at four gates: plan (A.2) · pack (A.3) · critique (A.6) · walk (A.7). Each gate reads this matrix as the source of truth for "is this still distinct enough" — **scored against five columns, not three**.

## 2 · How to use the matrix at intake

At workflow A.1 (intake), the orchestrator fills out a **draft row** for the new sibling across all 12 dimensions, alongside the **five existing rows** (NOT three). Then:

1. For each dimension, mark **distinct (✓)** or **collides (✗)** vs each of the five existing siblings.
2. Tally axes-different per existing sibling (e.g., "vs Pragma: 5/5 · vs Cornice: 5/5 · vs Fiscus: 5/5 · vs Solaria: 5/5 · vs Continua: 5/5"). **Five passes, not three.**
3. Apply DISTINCTNESS_RULES §1: **≥ 4 of 5 axes must differ vs every existing sibling** (the five axes there are voice · palette · imagery · typography · structure — they map to dimensions 1, 2-3, 6, 4, 8 here).
4. Apply CS-LAYOUT-12: **≥ 4 of 9 layout dimensions (L1..L9) must differ vs every existing sibling**, with the documented exception in §4 below for in-family near-occupants.
5. If any pair scores ≤ 3/5 on skin axes OR <4/9 on layout axes (without the documented exception), re-spec the offending dimensions before plan sign-off.
6. The filled matrix is appended to the planner brief and copied verbatim into the new template's content-module docstring (CS-EXEC-02).

## 4 · The accepted in-family near-occupant exception (Pragma ↔ Fiscus 2/9 § decision · 2026-05-03)

CS-LAYOUT-12 originally read "≥ 4 of 9 layout dimensions different between any pair." The Pragma↔Fiscus pair scores **2/9** (differ on L2 = `A` vs `A+slot4` and L3 = `absent` vs `slot-4` only). This was deferred at every flip pass since the divergence plan (2026-04-29) and is now formally addressed.

**§ Decision (2026-05-03)**: ACCEPT the 2/9 score. CS-LAYOUT-12 is reworded to "**≥ 4 of 9 different OR a documented in-family near-occupant relationship**." The Pragma↔Fiscus relationship is the first (and presently only) documented near-occupant pair. Rationale, evidence, and the wording change are filed in `factory/reports/hardening/corporate-suite-layout-family-matrix.md §6` and `factory/reports/hardening/corporate-suite-layout-variance-rules.md §2 (CS-LAYOUT-12)`.

**Operational consequence at this matrix**:
- A 6th sibling that scores 2/9 vs an existing sibling does NOT inherit the exception by default. The exception is named per-pair and requires its own § decision filed at the variance rules.
- The 6th sibling MUST score ≥ 4/9 vs each of the five live siblings unless the orchestrator files a fresh in-family near-occupant § decision at intake (workflow A.1) and ratifies it at A.6.
- Pragma and Fiscus remain a single near-occupant pair. Any 7th sibling that creates a **second** near-occupant pair triggers a § decision review on CS-LAYOUT-12 itself (the rule's intent is a single-exception ladder, not a generalised relaxation).

**Why Option C (formal acceptance) over Options A or B (migrate Pragma's L7 or Fiscus's L9)**:
- The argument is genuinely available: Pragma and Fiscus *are* both institutional advisory chrome variants (multi-partner organisation · serif-h1-LEFT split-55-45 hero · 5-link sticky-top navbar · 3-col footer). A reader examining the two side by side at 1920 sees two corporate-advisory firms whose differentiator is the calendar, and that is the right read — Fiscus's slot-4 cycle IS the family's identity, and the 2/9 score is what that identity costs structurally.
- The existing rule (CS-LAYOUT-12 ≥4/9) was written before LF-3 was understood as "LF-1 + slot-4," and it over-fits when the family difference is precisely a single-cell addition.
- Options A and B re-open a sibling for a layout migration, which is itself a workflow A.5+ pass with frozen-sibling regression risk on the other 4 — exactly the multi-session diversion this hardening pass is avoiding.
- Options A and B remain available later if the orchestrator decides Option C was wrong.

The decision is binding. Future workstreams that read this matrix at intake apply the 4/9 rule with the named near-occupant ladder at §4 — they do not silently waive the rule.
