## 4 · Risky overlaps that need active management

These are the dimensions where Continua sits closer to a sibling than ideal. Each is mitigated below; the build phase must verify the mitigation lands.

### Risk 1 · Cool-on-cool palette echo of Pragma (palette adjacency)
- Pragma: navy + cool-blue secondary + emerald accent (full cool)
- Continua: deep pine + pewter secondary + brass accent (cool-secondary + warm-accent)
- **Why this is not a collision**: Pragma's is FULL cool · Continua's introduces a warm accent (brass) which Pragma does not have. The brass is the chrome's only colored interactive element (CTA · focus ring) and is highly visible. A first-30s read of Pragma vs Continua is "navy/emerald institutional advisory" vs "pine/brass stewardship office" — the warm accent lands as the differentiator.
- **Mitigation**: ensure the brass accent appears at ≥ 3 viewport touchpoints (focus ring · trailing nav CTA · KPI eyebrow tints · CTA-closer fill) so a viewer cannot miss it.
- **Walk gate to verify**: contrast report at 1920/1280/720 confirms brass accent visible against pine + cream both.

### Risk 2 · Object-led hero reading too close to Fiscus's desk-with-documents
- Fiscus's hero: tidy desk + documents + laptop + eyeglasses · warm-neutral
- Continua's hero: stewardship reading-room with archive cabinet · brass key on cabinet face · mahogany-warm
- **Why this is not a collision**: Fiscus shows MULTIPLE document layers (papers, laptop, eyeglasses) framed at desk-task scale. Continua shows a SINGLE archival object (cabinet · key) framed at room-architectural scale. Fiscus reads "tax pro at work today" · Continua reads "stewardship office across years."
- **Mitigation**: hero photo selection at A.3 must explicitly reject any frame that shows >1 document or any keyboard/laptop. The cabinet brass detail must be the focal point.
- **Walk gate to verify**: imagery coherence check at 1280 and 1024 (where object-led photos most often crop into incoherence).

### Risk 3 · Stakeholder summary "stewardship family office" reads close to Pragma's "institutional advisory"
- Pragma stakeholder one-liner: "boardroom-grade B2B advisory"
- Continua: "stewardship-grade family office · multi-gen continuity"
- **Why this is not a collision**: Pragma is B2B (corporate clients). Continua is families (private clients). Pragma is mandate-per-engagement. Continua is mandate-across-generations. The audience and time horizon both differ.
- **Mitigation**: home page surfaces "famiglia / generazioni / continuità / patto familiare" in the first scroll (h1, meta-strip, pillars, sectors-ribbon label) so the B2C/B2family positioning lands within 30 seconds.
- **Walk gate to verify**: the "remove the studio name" test (CS-TONE-05) — page must read as a real family office, not as "any stewardship firm." Reviewer reads the home with the wordmark hidden and confirms the audience reads as families.

### Risk 4 · Leadership block + photo-present + 60s/40s feels close to Solaria's photo-present leadership (the only photo-present cluster precedent)
- Solaria: 1 coach + 1 associato (2 stewards) · 30s × 2 Caucasian (the matrix-flagged demographic gap)
- Continua: 3 stewards · 60s + 40s · explicit visible diversity
- **Why this is not a collision**: count differs (3 vs 2) · age band differs (60s+40s vs 30s+30s) · diversity intent named (Continua explicit, Solaria flagged as gap)
- **Mitigation**: at A.3 pack curation, reject any portrait frame that places senior steward in 30s band; reject any pair that reads as same-demographic.
- **Walk gate to verify**: reviewer at A.7 confirms 3 distinct demographics across leadership cards.

## 6 · The visible moves that create true sibling distinctness

If a stakeholder spent 30 seconds on each of the four pages (Pragma, Fiscus, Solaria, Continua), these are the moves that would make Continua read as the 4th distinct identity rather than a recolored 3rd.

1. **Object-led hero with zero people** — the moment a viewer sees Pragma/Fiscus/Solaria all having visible humans (boardroom · implied solo · 1:1) and Continua showing an archive cabinet alone, the cluster's silhouette breaks open. This is the single most decisive move.

2. **Pine + brass instead of any blue or any earth tone** — the cluster has been cool-blue (Pragma), warm-gold (Fiscus), warm-earth (Solaria). Pine green is the first cool-non-blue primary. Brass is warm but reads "weighed metal" not "tech green" or "ocra." A stakeholder cycling through the four sees palette as the second-strongest distinguisher.

3. **The mid-strip names a CADENCE, not a number or a calendar event** — Continua's mid-strip says "Cadenza CdF · 4 riunioni / anno." Fiscus's says "scadenza fiscale 31 marzo." Solaria's says "inizio dichiarato → cadenza → fine dichiarata." Pragma has none. Continua introduces a NEW kind of mid-strip beat: the firm's RHYTHM rather than a deadline or a process arc.

4. **The CTA names a DIALOGUE, not a call** — "Avvia un dialogo di mandato" reads as the start of a multi-year conversation, not a meeting on Tuesday. Pragma/Fiscus/Solaria all sell a call/appointment; Continua sells the opening of a relationship.

5. **Public Sans in the body** — the moment a body-text comparison happens, Continua's Public Sans reads visibly different from Pragma/Solaria's Inter and Fiscus's Plex Sans. The cluster's "Inter on whatever serif" risk is closed.

6. **Crimson Pro h1 italic on a single temporal noun** — Pragma's italic falls on agency words ("decisioni"), Fiscus's on imperatives ("adempimento"), Solaria's on contrast nouns ("terapia"/"consulenza"). Continua's italic falls on a TIME word ("generazioni"). The italic emphasis carries the firm's defining axis (time horizon), not its method or its product.

7. **Leadership block with 60s + 40s + visible diversity, photo-present** — the only photo-present leadership precedent is Solaria's (30sCx2 gap). Continua takes the "photo-present" precedent forward and explicitly fixes the demographic gap — visible age + gender + ethnicity diversity becomes the differentiator within the same precedent.

8. **Cases labelled "Mandati in continuità" with multi-year duration markers** — Pragma's NDA-anonymized + Fiscus's "Casi seguiti" + Solaria's "Casi anonimizzati" all read as past-tense closure. Continua's "Mandati in continuità" reads as ongoing, multi-year, with explicit duration. The label itself signals time-axis instead of outcome-list.

9. **Whistleblowing link prominent in the legal row** — required for advisory clusters by D.lgs. 24/2023 (CS-FOOT-02). Continua's footer treats this as a first-class link, not a footnote — signals the public-trust posture that family-office stewardship requires.

10. **The "remove the studio name" test passes for a stewardship office specifically** — delete "Continua" from every surface and the page still reads as a real stewardship family office, not as a generic "premium firm." The vocabulary (CdF, custodi, patto familiare, generazioni, mandato in continuità, Albo Trustees, STEP) is structurally distinct from the boardroom · commercialista · coaching vocabularies of the other three.
